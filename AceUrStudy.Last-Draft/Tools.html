 Feynman Technique


The Feynman Technique is a study method designed to simplify learning and enhance understanding. Start by choosing a topic and explaining it in your own words, as if teaching someone with no prior knowledge. Focus on clarity and avoid using complex terms. If you struggle to explain a part, go back to the source material and learn it again until you can simplify it. Once done, review and refine your explanation, ensuring it’s clear and concise.

❖ This approach helps you identify knowledge gaps, solidify understanding,
and improve your ability to recall and apply information effectively.



 Blurring Technique


The blurring method is a study technique where you focus on the most important concepts while skipping unnecessary details. This approach helps reduce overwhelm and improves retention by prioritizing key ideas. To enhance this method, follow these tips:

❯ Don’t study one thing for too long to maintain your focus and energy.
❯ Break your study sessions into short intervals of 20-30 minutes for better concentration.
❯ Write down the information you’ve learned to reinforce understanding and memory.
❯ This technique prevents boredom and allows you to learn more efficiently while staying engaged.

  Pomodoro Technique


The Pomodoro Technique is a popular time management method designed to boost productivity and focus during study sessions. It involves breaking study time into short intervals, typically 25 minutes, called Pomodoros, followed by a 5-minute break. After completing four Pomodoros, take a longer break, around 15-30 minutes.

❖ This method helps maintain high levels of concentration while preventing
burnout, as it encourages regular rest periods. By using the Pomodoro Technique, students can enhance their focus, improve time management, and avoid procrastination, making studying more efficient and less overwhelming.
